Cribbage Card Game

Players: 2–4 (best with 2)

Time: 30–60 minutes

Ages: 10+

Type: Card / Pegging / Strategy

🎯 Objective

Be the first to score 121 points by forming combinations during play and hand scoring, using a cribbage board and pegs to track progress.

🧰 Setup

  1. Use a standard 52-card deck (no jokers).
  2. Each player receives:
    • 6 cards (2 players)
    • 5 cards (3–4 players)
  3. Each player discards 2 cards to the crib (dealer’s extra hand).
  4. Cut the deck to reveal the starter card (face-up).
    If it's a Jack, the dealer scores 2 points ("His Heels").

🎮 Gameplay Phases

1. The Play (Pegging Phase)

  • Players alternate playing one card at a time, forming a running total (max 31).
  • Scoring during play:
    • 15 exactly = 2 points
    • 31 exactly = 2 points
    • Pairs = 2 points
    • Triplets = 6 points
    • Four of a Kind = 12 points
    • Runs of 3+ = points equal to run length
  • If no one can play without going over 31, declare "Go."
  • After a 31 or Go, reset total to 0 and continue with remaining cards.

2. Hand Scoring

  • Each player scores their hand plus the starter card.
  • The dealer also scores the crib plus starter.

🃏 Scoring Combinations

  • Fifteen: Any combo that adds to 15 = 2 points
  • Pairs: 2 points
  • Runs: 3+ cards in sequence = length in points
  • Flush: 4 same-suit cards in hand = 4 points
    +1 if starter matches = 5 points
  • "His Nobs": Jack in hand matching starter suit = 1 point

📦 Crib Rules

  • The crib is an extra hand for the dealer.
  • Scored with the same rules as player hands.
  • Only the dealer benefits from crib points.

🏁 Ending and Winning

  • First player to reach or exceed 121 points wins.
  • You can win during either the play phase or the hand scoring phase.
  • No points may be scored after an opponent reaches 121.

📝 Notes

  • Use a cribbage board with two pegs per player for leapfrog scorekeeping.
  • Cut the deck to determine who deals first — low card wins.
  • Strategy involves balancing your hand and crib potential when discarding.
