Farkle Dice Game

Manufactured by: Play Monster

Players: 2 or more (best with 3–8)

Time: 30–45 minutes

Ages: 8+

Type: Dice / Press-your-luck / Scoring

🎯 Objective

Be the first player to reach 10,000 points by rolling scoring combinations of dice.

🧰 Setup

  1. You need 6 standard dice.
  2. Each player needs a scoresheet and pencil.
  3. Choose who goes first (e.g. highest roll).
  4. Play proceeds clockwise.

🎲 Gameplay

  • Roll all 6 dice on your turn.
  • After each roll, set aside at least one scoring die or combination.
  • You may choose to stop and bank points, or roll the remaining dice.
  • If no scoring dice appear, it’s a Farkle — you lose all points for that turn.
  • If all 6 dice score, you get a hot dice bonus and may roll all 6 again.

💯 Scoring Combinations

  • Single 1 = 100 points
  • Single 5 = 50 points
  • Three 1s = 1000 points
  • Three 2s–6s = 100 × face value
  • Four of a kind = 1000 points
  • Five of a kind = 2000 points
  • Six of a kind = 3000 points
  • Straight (1–6) = 1500 points
  • Three pairs = 1500 points
  • Four of a kind + a pair = 1500 points
  • Two triplets = 2500 points

🧮 Getting on the Board

You must score at least 500 points in a single turn to start banking points.

🏁 Ending and Winning

  • First player to reach 10,000 triggers the final round.
  • All other players get one last turn.
  • Highest total score wins.

📝 Notes

  • Always set aside at least one scoring die per roll.
  • Push your luck to build a big turn—but don’t Farkle!
  • Optional rule: 3 consecutive Farkles = -1000 point penalty.

🔀 Variations

  • Opening Score: Many play with a 500-point opening score requirement to bank.
  • Scoring Dice Variants:
    • “Pocket Farkle” scores three 1s as 300 instead of 1000.
    • Some versions offer huge bonuses for 1s in 4+, 5+, or 6-of-a-kind.
  • Triple Farkle Penalty: Some use a -1000 penalty for 3 turns in a row with no score.
  • Charity Roll: Some allow each opponent one extra roll after someone reaches 10,000.
    (This rule is popular but debated — it can add drama or feel messy.)
