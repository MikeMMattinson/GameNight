SKUNK Dice Game

Players: 2+

Time: 15–30 minutes

Ages: 8+

Type: Dice / Risk-taking / Probability

🎯 Objective

Score the most points by deciding when to stop rolling dice—without losing your points!

🧰 Setup

  1. Each player gets a scoresheet with 5 columns labeled: S, K, U, N, K
  2. Two dice are needed
  3. One player is the roller and rolls for everyone
  4. All players start with 0 points

🎲 Gameplay

The game is played over 5 rounds — one for each letter in SKUNK.

  1. For each round:
    • The roller rolls both dice.
    • Before each roll, everyone decides whether to stay in or drop out.
    • If you stay in, you’re at risk but can keep scoring.
    • Points = sum of the two dice, added to that round’s total.
    • If a 1 is rolled on either die:
      • You lose all points for that round and are out for the rest of it.
    • If double 1s are rolled:
      • You lose all points for that round and all previous rounds.
  2. At any point during the round, a player may “sit down” (drop out) and lock in their score for that round.
  3. Proceed to the next round/letter after everyone drops out or Farkles.

🏁 Ending and Winning

  • After all 5 rounds (S–K–U–N–K) are complete
  • Add up each player’s locked-in round scores
  • Highest total score wins

📝 Notes

  • Great for teaching risk vs. reward and probability
  • Rotate the roller each round or use the same roller all game
  • Use checkmarks, coins, or tokens to track who's still in each round
