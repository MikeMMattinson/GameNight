Liverpool Rummy: “Down & Out”

Game Setup

  • Players: 3–5 players
  • Decks: 2 standard decks with Jokers (108 cards total)
  • Objective: Finish with the lowest total score

Terminology

  • Set: 3+ cards of the same rank (e.g., 4♦ 4♠ 4♣)
  • Run: 4+ cards of the same suit in sequence (e.g., 5♣ 6♣ 7♣ 8♣)
  • Ace can be high (J–Q–K–A) or low (A–2–3–4), but no wrapping (K–A–2–3 is NOT allowed)
  • Wild Cards: Jokers and 2s are wild, but cannot be adjacent within a set or run

Gameplay Rules

  • Player's Turn:
    • Draw from deck or discard pile (only if not already down)
    • Go down if round requirement is met
    • Add to any melds
    • Discard (cannot discard a wild card unless a house rule allows it)
  • Going Down: Lay down all sets and/or runs in one turn when round requirement is met
  • Going Out: Lay down and play all cards with no discard on final move
  • Adding to Melds: Once down, players may add to any melds (their own or others')
  • Taking Out of Turn: If current player declines face-up card, others may take it with a penalty draw

Scoring the Hand

When a player goes out, remaining cards are scored:

  • Joker = 50 points
  • 2 = 20 points
  • Ace = 15 points
  • 8–King = 10 points
  • 3–7 = 5 points

Record scores in the game journal.

Optional House Rules

  • Exact Deal Bonus: Dealer gets -50 points if deal is exact
  • Limit on Taking Out of Turn: 3x per player, or no limit
  • Discard Wild Cards:
    • Allowed for all players
    • Not allowed
    • Allowed only for last player to go down
  • Lindsey Bonus Rule: If a 2 is used naturally in a run (e.g., A♣ 2♣ 3♣ 4♣), reduce score by 10 points
